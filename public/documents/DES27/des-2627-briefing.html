Why has it jumped so much? This is not a straightforward pay uplift. The rate has increased for two reasons acting together: the eligibility restriction has been removed (see below), and NHS England has raised the salary ceiling from the lower quartile to the top of the salaried GP pay range — because experienced GPs earn significantly more than newly qualified ones.

A standard full-time salaried GP works 9 sessions/week, each session being 4 hours 10 minutes. Based on 43 working weeks/year (allowing for 6 weeks annual leave, bank holidays and study leave) = 387 sessions/year. On-costs: 15% employer NI + 23.7% NHS pension. PCNs claim actual salary + on-costs — £152,900 is the ceiling, not a flat rate.

⚠️
Important caveat — budget hasn't risen to match

The ARRS pot has only increased by 3.1% per weighted patient. Recruiting an experienced GP at or near £152,900 will consume a very large share of a PCN's total ARRS allocation — potentially at the expense of other roles. This is a strategic trade-off, not a free uplift.

ℹ️
London weighting

A London-weighted maximum rate for GP ARRS also applies. See Table 3c in the DES specification for the full figure (£155,698).

GP ARRS

Who you can — and can't — claim for

✓

You CAN claim for

  • Any GP not substantively employed in a Core Network Practice within the previous 12 months
  • Experienced GPs — the 2-year post-CCT restriction is removed from April 2026
  • GPs who were engaged or employed on a temporary basis (e.g. to cover maternity or sickness leave) — this is not considered substantive employment
  • A GP already claimed under 2025/26 ARRS rules who continues in the same post — the claim can simply continue
  • GPs employed by a third-party organisation (e.g. federation) that provides the service to the PCN
✕

You CANNOT claim for

  • A GP who has been substantively employed in any Core Network Practice of the PCN within the previous 12 months
  • A GP being used to backfill an existing GP post that was funded before 31 March 2019
  • Any GP where the PCN cannot demonstrate additionality — the role must be above the 2019 baseline
  • A GP where a registration number has not been provided on the ARRS portal claim (new 2026/27 requirement)
What counts as "substantive employment"?
The DES specification is explicit: a GP engaged or employed on a temporary basis — for example to cover maternity leave, sickness leave or a short-term vacancy — is not considered to have been substantively employed. Only those in permanent or ongoing salaried roles are caught by the 12-month rule.
🚫
New from 2026/27 — registration numbers on claims

All professionally regulated ARRS staff — including GPs — must have their GMC registration number included on the mandatory online ARRS portal claim. Claims without a registration number will not be compliant.

New in 2026/27

Non-direct patient care ARRS roles

Section 7.3.2-C
For the first time, PCNs can claim ARRS reimbursement for non-direct patient care roles — operational, administrative, and enabling roles — where agreed with the commissioner. This is a significant new flexibility introduced in 2026/27.
Maximum reimbursable amount The rate is agreed with the commissioner and must be commensurate with the scope of the role — up to a hard ceiling of Band 8c (£109,943) nationally. There is no fixed table rate; the PCN proposes, the commissioner agrees.
The five tests — commissioner can require evidence on all of these
  1. Additional — the role must be genuinely new headcount, not an existing post being transferred to ARRS funding.
  2. Demonstrably different — the role must not duplicate another ARRS role already available to the PCN.
  3. Clear scope of practice — a written role description with appropriate training requirements must exist.
  4. No duplication — does not replicate existing local provision, unless a clear local need can be demonstrated.
  5. Rate appropriate to scope — salary must be proportionate to the role, up to Band 8c maximum.

Examples of roles likely to qualify

NEW POST
PCN Operations Manager (additional)

A second operational manager post — distinct from an existing PCN Manager — covering specific workstreams such as Enhanced Access, NRES coordination, or INT delivery. Band 7–8a range. Requires commissioner agreement and a clear scope.

NEW POST
Population Health / Data Analyst

A role focused on analytics, reporting, and population health intelligence to support PCN delivery and INT planning. Band 6–7. Strong case given the new digital risk stratification requirements in 2026/27.

NEW POST
Workforce / HR Coordinator

A dedicated post supporting PCN HR, recruitment, induction, and ARRS compliance — particularly relevant where ARRS headcount is growing. Band 5–6.

NEW POST
Communications & Engagement Lead

A post covering patient communications, Enhanced Access promotion, and community engagement — supporting the new GPSS requirements and neighbourhood health agenda. Band 5–6.

NEW POST
Finance / Governance Officer

Supporting ARRS claims, PCN financial reporting, and governance documentation — distinct from practice finance roles. Band 5–6.

🚫
What you cannot do — the additionality trap

You cannot switch an existing, funded post onto ARRS under 7.3.2-C. A PCN Manager, Operations Lead, or admin role that has been employed and funded by the PCN since 2020 (or any year) is not additional — it is an existing cost being subsidised. The spec is explicit that deliberate attempts to subvert additionality may be referred for fraud investigation.

⚠️
Process — commissioner agreement is not optional

Unlike most ARRS roles, 7.3.2-C requires advance commissioner agreement before recruitment. Engage your ICB early, present the five-test evidence, and get written sign-off before committing to a hire. Do not recruit and then seek retrospective approval.

Contract Requirements

New & changed requirements

ENDED
PCN Grouping for vaccine sharing — abolished

The PCN Grouping mechanism that allowed flu/COVID vaccines to be shared between member practices ends 1 April 2026. Subcontracting or agency arrangements must be in place. This is an immediate operational action.

NEW
Flu/COVID vaccination in Aligned Care Homes

Where Core Network Practices participate in the flu/COVID Enhanced Service, the PCN must ensure all Aligned Care Home residents are offered vaccination. The Network Agreement must be updated to document collaborative arrangements.

NEW
Cancer screening uptake duties

Explicit new duties to improve uptake of breast, cervical, bowel, lung, diabetic eye, AAA, and antenatal/newborn screening — including NHS App signposting. Also a new duty to review cancer referral quality against NICE Guideline 12.

NEW
Digital risk stratification for continuity of care

PCNs should now use digital risk stratification tools to identify patients who would benefit most from continuity of care — including named GP allocation where appropriate.

NEW
Vaccination status review for care home residents

PCNs must review the vaccination status of all Aligned Care Home residents and notify the registered practice of any gaps in routine vaccination programmes.

NEW
GPSS survey — third-party employers

All ARRS staff must participate in the GP Staff Survey. Where employed by a third-party organisation, the employer must provide that individual's email address to the PCN's GPSS key contact.

CLARIFIED
Neighbourhood alignment — limited safety net

Where a commissioner (with local authority) defines a neighbourhood not aligned with a PCN's Network Area, the PCN must collaborate on alignment. Described explicitly as limited — not widespread reconfiguration.

Board Actions Required

What needs to happen now

1
Immediate — April 2026 Care home vaccine sharing

Confirm any flu/COVID vaccine sharing between practices is formalised via subcontracting or agency arrangement. PCN Grouping ends 1 April 2026.

2
From April 2026 ARRS portal — registration numbers

Ensure registration numbers for all regulated ARRS staff (GPs, pharmacists, physios, etc.) are captured and included in monthly portal claims.

3
GPSS — ongoing Third-party employer email addresses

Where ARRS staff are employed by third parties (e.g. federation, NRES), ensure those employers provide individual email addresses to the PCN's GPSS key contact.

4
Strategic — Q1/Q2 GP ARRS — review your position

With experienced GPs now eligible, consider whether recruiting an ARRS GP makes sense for the PCN. Be aware this will significantly reduce headroom for other ARRS roles within the overall pot.

5
Strategic — Q1/Q2 Non-direct patient care ARRS roles

Review whether operational or admin roles could now be claimed via ARRS under Section 7.3.2-C. Commissioner agreement required; additionality principles apply.

6
By 31 October 2026 Workforce Plan submission

A completed PCN Workforce Plan must be submitted to the commissioner by 31 October 2026. Begin review of staffing mix early to allow preparation time.

7
Population health — ongoing Cancer screening & referral review

Incorporate new screening uptake duties and cancer referral quality review (NICE Guideline 12) into PCN population health management planning and INT discussions.
