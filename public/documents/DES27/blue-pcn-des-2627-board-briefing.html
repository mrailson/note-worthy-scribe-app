The biggest change

The structural shift Board members should understand

Money moves out of the PCN

£246k of CASP + CAIP at Blue PCN is gone.
£241k returns — but flows direct to our six practices.

−£175,838

Net PCN-level reduction. The £292m nationally repurposed from the Capacity & Access Payment now lands at practice level as a GP Reimbursement Scheme, tied specifically to same-day urgent access delivery.

Why this matters operationally

Previously, CASP and CAIP were PCN-level payments — they flowed into the PCN's central pot and the Board decided how to deploy them (shared staff, workflow improvements, same-day access schemes, etc.).

From April 2026, the equivalent funding is ring-fenced at practice level and tied to a specific purpose: employing additional GP sessions to support clinically urgent same-day access.

The Board no longer controls this money. Practices do.

Why this matters strategically

Blue PCN's central delivery model (e.g. Enhanced Access, shared workflow, CAIP-funded initiatives) loses ~£176k of flexible funding.

Meanwhile, practices gain ~£47k each on average — but can only use it for GP capacity for urgent same-day demand.

Key question for the Board: how do we coordinate practice-level GP Reimbursement decisions so that collective same-day access capacity is coherent across the six practices?

⚠️

ICB application route for well-staffed practices

The GP Contract letter states that practices with a high GP-to-patient ratio must apply to their ICB to access this funding — this is not automatic. Park Avenue & Kings Heath and Bugbrooke (our largest practices) should review their GP WTE per 1,000 patients before April to anticipate whether an ICB application will be required.

Why the jump?

This is not a straightforward pay uplift. Two things happened together:

  • The 2-year post-CCT restriction is removed — experienced GPs now qualify.
  • The salary ceiling moved from the lower quartile of the salaried GP pay range to the top of that range — reflecting that experienced GPs earn more.

Per session, the maximum is ~£286/session (9 sessions/wk × 43 wks = 387 sessions).

⚠️

The trade-off — our ARRS pot only grew by 3.1%

Blue PCN's total ARRS envelope rose by ~£41k (to £1.37m). Recruiting one experienced ARRS GP at or near the £152,900 maximum would consume over 11% of our entire ARRS budget. This is a strategic choice — it comes directly at the expense of other roles we might otherwise fund.

✓ We CAN claim for

  • Any GP not substantively employed in a Blue PCN Core Network Practice in the previous 12 months
  • Experienced GPs — the 2-year post-CCT restriction is removed
  • GPs engaged on a temporary basis (e.g. maternity/sickness cover)
  • GPs already claimed under 2025/26 ARRS rules — the claim simply continues
  • GPs employed by a third-party (e.g. federation) delivering service to the PCN

✕ We CANNOT claim for

  • A GP substantively employed in any Blue PCN practice within the previous 12 months
  • A GP backfilling an existing post funded before 31 March 2019
  • Any GP where we cannot demonstrate additionality (above 2019 baseline)
  • A GP whose GMC registration number is not provided on the ARRS portal claim NEW

New in 2026/27

Non-direct patient care ARRS roles (Section 7.3.2-C)

For the first time, Blue PCN can claim ARRS reimbursement for non-direct patient care roles — operational, administrative, and enabling roles — subject to commissioner agreement and five tests.

Ceiling: Band 8c (£109,943 incl. on-costs) nationally. No fixed table rate — the PCN proposes, the commissioner agrees.

The five tests the commissioner will apply

  1. Additional — genuinely new headcount, not an existing post transferred to ARRS.
  2. Demonstrably different — does not duplicate another ARRS role already available.
  3. Clear scope of practice — written role description with training requirements.
  4. No local duplication — unless a clear local need can be demonstrated.
  5. Rate appropriate to scope — proportionate to the role, up to Band 8c max.

🚫

The additionality trap — do not move Claire's post onto ARRS

Blue PCN's existing PCN Manager and operational roles (funded since PCN formation) are not additional under 7.3.2-C — they are existing costs. Deliberate attempts to subvert additionality may be referred for fraud investigation. The rule: new money for new posts, not subsidy for existing ones.

⚠️

Commissioner agreement is mandatory — not retrospective

Unlike other ARRS roles, 7.3.2-C requires advance written commissioner agreement before recruitment. We should engage NHS Northamptonshire ICB early with the five-test evidence and secure sign-off before committing to any hire.

Contract requirements

New & changed requirements

ENDED

PCN Grouping for vaccine sharing — abolished

The PCN Grouping mechanism that allowed flu/COVID vaccines to be shared between Blue PCN member practices ends 1 April 2026. Subcontracting or agency arrangements must be in place. This is an immediate operational action for the Board.

NEW

Flu/COVID vaccination in Aligned Care Homes (our 349 beds)

Where Blue PCN Core Network Practices participate in the flu/COVID Enhanced Service, the PCN must ensure all Aligned Care Home residents are offered vaccination. Our Network Agreement must be updated to document collaborative arrangements.

NEW

Cancer screening uptake duties

Explicit new duties to improve uptake of breast, cervical, bowel, lung, diabetic eye, AAA, and antenatal/newborn screening — including NHS App signposting. Also a new duty to review cancer referral quality against NICE Guideline 12.

NEW

Digital risk stratification for continuity of care

Blue PCN should now use digital risk stratification tools to identify patients who would benefit most from continuity of care — including named GP allocation where appropriate.

NEW

Vaccination status review for care home residents

Blue PCN must review the vaccination status of all 349 Aligned Care Home residents and notify the registered practice of any gaps in routine vaccination programmes.

NEW

GPSS survey — third-party employers

All ARRS staff must participate in the GP Staff Survey. Where employed by a third-party organisation, the employer must provide that individual's email address to Blue PCN's GPSS key contact.

CLARIFIED

Neighbourhood alignment — limited safety net

Where a commissioner (with local authority) defines a neighbourhood not aligned with our Network Area, the PCN must collaborate on alignment. Described explicitly as limited — not widespread reconfiguration.
