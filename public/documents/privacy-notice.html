Controller and processor

Notewell AI is deployed by NHS primary care organisations (GP practices and Primary Care Networks). For data protection purposes:

  • The NHS organisation deploying Notewell AI (your GP practice or PCN) is the data controller — they determine the purposes and means of processing personal data relating to their patients and staff.
  • PCN Services Ltd acts as the data processor — we process personal data only on the documented instructions of the deploying NHS organisation, under a Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

This notice is provided by PCN Services Ltd as processor. Your employing or registered NHS organisation should also provide you with their own privacy notice explaining how they use Notewell AI as controller.

What Notewell AI does

Notewell AI is a multi-module AI-powered platform designed for NHS primary care staff. It helps clinical and administrative staff work more efficiently. It is not a patient-facing application — it is used exclusively by authorised NHS staff.

The platform comprises five modules:

📋 Meeting Manager

AI-assisted meeting transcription, summarisation and action tracking for PCN and practice governance meetings.

📨 Complaints Manager

Structured AI-assisted workflow for managing NHS complaints in line with NHS England guidance.

📄 Policy Generator

AI-assisted creation and maintenance of NHS-compliant clinical and operational policies.

💬 Ask AI

Conversational AI assistant providing responses to clinical and operational queries from healthcare staff.

🎙 Voice Assistant

Real-time AI voice support for clinical and administrative queries, powered by ElevenLabs.

Important: Notewell AI is a decision-support tool. All AI-generated outputs must be reviewed and approved by a qualified healthcare professional before being acted upon. The platform does not make autonomous clinical decisions.

Special Category data

The Complaints Manager module and the Ask AI / Voice Assistant modules may process Special Category personal data under Article 9 UK GDPR — specifically health data relating to patients. This data receives enhanced protection and is only processed where:

  • the processing is necessary for the provision of health or social care (Article 9(2)(h) UK GDPR), or
  • the processing is necessary for reasons of public interest in the area of public health (Article 9(2)(i) UK GDPR).

Data we do not process

Notewell AI does not:

  • access or connect to GP clinical systems (EMIS, SystmOne) automatically or in real time
  • process financial or payment data
  • process data relating to children unless incidentally referenced in a complaint or clinical query by a staff user
  • use personal data for any commercial purpose, advertising or profiling
  • sell or rent personal data to any third party

How we use your data

PCN Services Ltd processes personal data only for the following purposes, and only as instructed by the deploying NHS organisation as controller:

  • To provide the Notewell AI platform — including all five modules — to authorised NHS staff at the deploying practice or PCN.
  • To authenticate users and maintain the security of the platform, including enforcing role-based access controls.
  • To maintain audit logs of user access and activity, in accordance with NHS DSPT and clinical safety requirements.
  • To process inputs and generate outputs via AI models, as directed by the user in real time. Inputs are not retained beyond the session unless explicitly saved by the user.
  • To support clinical safety monitoring — anonymised, aggregated usage data may be used to identify and address potential safety issues with AI outputs.
  • To provide technical support — support staff may access log data where necessary to diagnose a reported issue, subject to appropriate access controls.

We do not use personal data to train or improve AI models without the explicit consent of the deploying NHS organisation and, where applicable, the individuals concerned.

International data transfers

All core Notewell AI data is stored and processed within the United Kingdom.

The Voice Assistant module uses ElevenLabs voice synthesis technology. ElevenLabs may process voice audio data in the United States. This transfer is covered by:

  • an International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) between PCN Services Ltd and ElevenLabs, as required under UK GDPR for transfers to countries without UK adequacy status, and
  • a Transfer Risk Assessment (TRA) confirming the transfer is appropriate given the nature, context and purpose of the processing.

No other personal data is transferred outside the United Kingdom.

How we protect your data

PCN Services Ltd takes the security of personal data seriously. Technical and organisational measures in place include:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between users and the platform is encrypted using TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • Encryption at rest: All data stored on our infrastructure is encrypted at rest using AES-256.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA): MFA is mandatory for all user accounts and all privileged administrative access.
  • Role-based access control: Users can only access data within their authorised scope. Access rights are reviewed regularly.
  • Audit logging: All access to and activity within the platform is logged and available for review by the deploying organisation.
  • Secure development: The platform is developed in adherence to the DSIT/NCSC Software Security Code of Practice.
  • Penetration testing: Independent third-party penetration testing (OWASP Top 10, CVSS) is scheduled for completion by September 2026, prior to full programme rollout.
  • Staff training: All PCN Services Ltd staff with access to personal data receive data protection and information security training.
  • Incident response: A documented data breach response procedure is in place. We will notify the deploying NHS organisation (as controller) of any breach within 24 hours of discovery, to enable them to fulfil their ICO notification obligations within the 72-hour statutory window.

Your data protection rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. Because PCN Services Ltd acts as a data processor, requests relating to personal data held by the platform should be directed to the deploying NHS organisation (your GP practice or PCN) as the data controller. We will assist them in fulfilling your request.

Right of Access

You can request a copy of the personal data held about you (a Subject Access Request).

Right to Rectification

You can ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Right to Erasure

You can ask for your data to be deleted, subject to legal and regulatory retention requirements.

Right to Restrict Processing

You can ask us to restrict how we use your data in certain circumstances.

Right to Data Portability

You can ask for a copy of your data in a machine-readable format, where applicable.

Right to Object

You can object to processing based on legitimate interests or public interest grounds.

Rights re: Automated Decisions

You have rights in relation to automated decision-making and profiling. Notewell AI does not make fully automated decisions about individuals — all outputs require human review.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact your GP practice or PCN in the first instance. You can also contact PCN Services Ltd directly using the details in the Contact us section below.

Cookies

Notewell AI uses cookies and similar technologies to support the operation of the platform. We use:

  • Strictly necessary cookies — required for authentication, session management and security. These cannot be disabled.
  • Functional cookies — used to remember your preferences (e.g. language settings). These can be disabled but may affect how the platform works.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand how the platform is being used, to improve it. These are anonymised and aggregated. You can opt out.

We do not use advertising, tracking or third-party marketing cookies. A full cookie notice is available at gpnotewell.co.uk/cookies.
