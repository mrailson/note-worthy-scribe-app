New Mandatory Access Requirement from 1 April 2026 — Practices must not ask patients to call back on another day. All clinically urgent requests require a same-day response. Non-urgent requests must receive a next-steps update by end of next working day. Autonomy to assess urgency rests with the GP or appropriately trained clinician. Practices failing targets must engage ICB improvement support.

Workforce, Referrals & Pharmacy Interface
👩‍⚕️

Workforce & GP CapacityNew reimbursement scheme + ARRS expansion

£292m GP Reimbursement Scheme NEWReplaces PCN Capacity & Access Payment (CAP). ~£47k per practice to fund extra GP sessions or recruit additional GPs for same-day urgent access.
ARRS opens to all GPs NEWNo longer restricted to newly qualified GPs. Top reimbursement rate raised to top of salaried GP pay range + on-costs.
High GP:patient ratio practices TBC Must apply for reimbursement funding via ICB rather than receiving it automatically. Exact threshold not yet defined — further guidance expected in coming weeks. National average is currently ~1 GP per 2,200 patients (WTE), ranging from <1,500 to >3,000 by region.
Broader ARRS rolesPCNs can recruit a wider range of clinical roles where agreed with their commissioner.
GP Staff Survey participationPCNs must participate in the General Practice Staff Survey and share staff contact details with their ICB.
🏥

Advice & Guidance (A&G)Now embedded in core — Enhanced Service retired

A&G Enhanced Service retired CHANGETransitions from optional Enhanced Service into core contract funding from April 2026.
Mandatory before planned referralsPractices must use A&G prior to or instead of a planned care referral where clinically appropriate, and must follow locally agreed pathways.
Local referral pathways applyMust follow ICB-agreed pathways, including single point of access models. NHS England will issue guidance on triage and communication standards.
Trusts required to meet processing standardsNHS England asking trusts to achieve national operational standards to ensure timely specialist advice provision.
Aim: reduce unnecessary referralsSupports timely specialist input and helps manage planned care demand across the system.
💊

Community Pharmacy InterfaceNew patient choice & cross-interface communication requirements

Nominated pharmacy on new Rx NEWMust reconfirm patient's nominated pharmacy when issuing a new prescription. Not required for repeat prescriptions.
Full pharmacy choice on referral toolsAny tool used to refer into community pharmacy services must offer the full choice of pharmacy providers — not a restricted list.
Dedicated monitored email address NEWRequired for pharmacy to contact practice when GP Connect unavailable. Must be shared in the Directory of Services. An existing practice email can be used.
Supports pharmacy independent prescribingEmail channel covers activities not yet supported within GP Connect, including independent prescribing queries from pharmacists.

PCN Responsibilities, QOF & Contract Structure
🔗

PCN DES: Clarified DutiesVaccinations · Continuity · Neighbourhood · Cancer

1
Continuity of CareUse risk stratification tools to identify cohorts needing continuity. Embed as core PCN practice from April 2026.
2
Neighbourhood AlignmentCollaborate with ICB to align PCN geography to natural communities as defined by ICBs and local authorities.
3
Care Home VaccinationsMust identify eligible older adult care home residents for seasonal & routine vaccinations (COVID-19, flu). PCNs must ensure arrangements are in place — not necessarily administering directly.
4
Cancer ScreeningProactive responsibilities clarified — referrals, safety netting per NICE guidance. All practices must share data with Lung Cancer Screening Programme.
📋

PMS & Contract StructureSub-contracting alignment & finance

PMS aligned to GMS CHANGEPMS sub-contracting regulations updated to match GMS — commissioners can object where patient safety, financial risk or delivery of obligations may be affected.
Local Enhanced Services flexibilityICBs can commission LES via the GP contract or NHS Standard Contract as the contracting vehicle.
2.5% assumed pay upliftCore uplift includes 2.5% pay assumption; real-terms growth 1.4%. To be revisited following pay review body recommendations.
Updated specifications to followFull guidance and updated contract specifications will be published in the coming weeks by NHS England.
✅

QOF & Enhanced Services: Key Changes UPDATEDQuality & Outcomes Framework — clinical workload implications from April 2026

+18 QOF points (~£25m)New indicators aligned with NICE guidance on childhood vaccinations, diabetes, obesity and heart failure.
Childhood vaccination progress payments NEWPractices can earn points for progress toward thresholds (not just hitting them). Lower threshold: 5 percentage points above 2-year baseline. Especially benefits practices in deprived areas.
MMRV vaccine updateChildhood vaccination indicators updated to reflect the introduction of the MMRV vaccine (VI001, VI002, VI003).
Obesity Enhanced Service retired CHANGEReplaced by two new QOF targets — referrals into structured weight management programmes and medicines optimisation (including GLP-1 weight loss injections).
New diabetes indicatorRequires delivery of all 8 NICE recommended diabetes care processes.
Heart failure: '4 pillars' indicatorUpdated to reflect the NICE recommended four pillars of heart failure treatment.
RSV vaccination mandate NEWMust offer RSV vaccine to all registered older adult care home residents and patients over 80 not previously vaccinated. Item of Service fee per vaccination.
QOF indicator streamliningSeveral existing indicators combined to reduce bureaucracy and administrative burden on practices.
