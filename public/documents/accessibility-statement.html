Accessibility Statement

This accessibility statement applies to Notewell AI, provided by PCN Services Ltd at gpnotewell.co.uk. Notewell AI is a web-based platform for NHS primary care staff.

About this statement

PCN Services Ltd wants as many people as possible to be able to use Notewell AI. We are committed to making this platform accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

For example, that means you should be able to:

  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts using browser or device settings
  • zoom in up to 400% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the platform using a keyboard
  • listen to most of the platform using a screen reader (including the most recent versions of NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver and TalkBack)

AbilityNet has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

Compliance status

⚠ Partially compliant with WCAG 2.2 level AA

Notewell AI is partially compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.2 AA standard, due to the non-compliances and exemptions listed below. We are actively working to resolve all known issues ahead of full programme rollout.

This platform is a pilot deployment used exclusively by NHS primary care staff (GPs, Advanced Clinical Practitioners, Practice Managers and administrative staff) across seven GP practices in Northamptonshire. A formal WCAG 2.2 AA audit is scheduled for completion by September 2026.

Known accessibility issues

We know some parts of Notewell AI are not fully accessible. The following issues have been identified during internal review and user testing:

Issue WCAG Criterion Impact Target Fix
Some form fields do not have visible labels that are programmatically associated with the input 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A) Screen reader users may not receive sufficient context for some input fields June 2026
Colour contrast ratio below 4.5:1 on some secondary interface elements (e.g. placeholder text, helper labels) 1.4.3 Contrast Minimum (Level AA) Users with low vision may have difficulty reading secondary text elements June 2026
Some interactive components (e.g. dropdown menus, modal dialogs) are not fully keyboard navigable 2.1.1 Keyboard (Level A) Keyboard-only users may be unable to access some functionality without a mouse July 2026
Focus is not always visible when navigating with a keyboard 2.4.11 Focus Appearance (Level AA) Keyboard users may not be able to determine which element has focus July 2026
AI-generated text outputs are not structured with ARIA landmarks or headings 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A) Screen reader users may not be able to navigate AI output content efficiently August 2026
Error messages for form validation are not always announced to assistive technologies 4.1.3 Status Messages (Level AA) Users relying on screen readers may not receive notification of validation errors June 2026
Voice assistant module does not provide a text alternative for audio output 1.2.1 Audio-only (Level A) Users who are deaf or hard of hearing may not be able to access voice assistant responses without a text transcript September 2026
PDF documents generated by the platform may not be tagged for accessibility 1.3.1 Info and Relationships (Level A) PDF outputs may not be usable with screen readers September 2026

Disproportionate burden

PCN Services Ltd does not currently claim any accessibility requirements as a disproportionate burden under Regulation 7 of the Accessibility Regulations. We are committed to remediating all known issues within the timelines set out above.

Content not within the scope of the accessibility regulations

Third-party content and services that we do not control or have not commissioned — for example, content delivered via integrated AI model APIs from third-party providers — may not meet the same accessibility standards. We are working with our service providers to improve this where possible.

How we tested this platform

Notewell AI was tested by PCN Services Ltd using the following approach:

  • Internal review: Manual keyboard navigation testing and screen reader testing (NVDA with Chrome; VoiceOver with Safari on macOS) conducted March 2026.
  • Browser zoom testing: All modules tested at 200% and 400% zoom using Chrome and Edge.
  • Colour contrast checking: All interface elements assessed using the WebAIM Contrast Checker against WCAG 2.2 AA minimum ratios.
  • User testing: Usability testing conducted during the 10-practice initial NHS pilot with NHS primary care staff, including feedback on interface navigation.

A formal independent WCAG 2.2 AA audit by a third-party accessibility specialist is planned for July–August 2026, with results published and acted upon before full programme rollout in October 2026.

What we are doing to improve accessibility

PCN Services Ltd is committed to achieving full WCAG 2.2 AA compliance. Our improvement timeline is:

  • March 2026 — Completed Internal accessibility audit completed. Known issues documented in this statement.
  • April–May 2026 Remediation Sprint 1: Form labels, colour contrast, error message announcements. Target: address all Level A failures.
  • June–July 2026 Remediation Sprint 2: Keyboard navigation, focus visibility, ARIA landmark implementation across all modules.
  • July–August 2026 Independent third-party WCAG 2.2 AA audit commissioned. Audit findings acted upon.
  • September 2026 Voice assistant text transcript feature, PDF accessibility tagging completed. All Level AA issues resolved.
  • October 2026 Target: Full WCAG 2.2 AA compliance declared. Updated accessibility statement published.

This statement will be reviewed and updated at least annually, and following any significant changes to the platform.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations') in England, Scotland and Wales. The Equality Commission for Northern Ireland (ECNI) is responsible for enforcing these regulations in Northern Ireland.

If you are not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS).
