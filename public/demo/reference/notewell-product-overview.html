The Notewell story

From a meeting scribe
to a platform for primary care.

Notewell wasn't conceived in a boardroom. It was built from inside the system it serves — by a PCN Manager and Digital Transformation Lead, in response to problems the primary care workforce sees every day and has no time to solve.

Notewell began as a tool for something brutally ordinary — getting accurate minutes out of a long NHS meeting without the Practice Manager or PCN admin team spending their weekend typing them up. It worked. People asked for more.

What followed was a natural progression. If the scribe could handle a ninety-minute PCN board meeting, it could handle a clinical MDT. If it could handle an MDT, it could handle an AgeWell home visit with a frail patient — the kind of long, detailed, relationship-driven conversation that generates pages of care planning work that nobody has time to do. The same scribe. Tuned for frailty. Tuned for consent. Tuned for the way older patients actually speak.

From there, the platform grew outward. If the transcript contained the evidence for a Patient Support Plan, it contained the evidence for a PHQ-9, a GAD-7, a 6-CIT, an ONS-4, a FRAT score — all of which could be extracted structurally rather than re-keyed. If it could do that, it could populate the long tail of forms the primary care workforce quietly drowns in: Blue Badge, Attendance Allowance, DS1500, CHC Checklist, Council Tax SMI exemption.

In parallel, other burdens got their own modules. Policy Generator came from watching practices scramble before CQC inspections. Complaints Manager came from seeing the same letter written the same way a hundred times across the PCN. NRES and ENN Dashboards came from the impossible task of reporting neighbourhood-level performance across seven different clinical systems.

Everything Notewell does today started the same way: someone inside primary care asking "could we not just get the computer to do this bit?" — and getting a useful answer before the end of the week.

Design principle

No model lock-in. Every AI service is swappable behind a common interface, so Notewell can pick the right model for the job — or replace one without rebuilding the product around it.

Safety & governance

NHS-grade by design.
Not bolted on after the fact.

Every output is draft-for-signature until a human signs it. Every extraction is traceable back to the transcript turn that evidenced it. Every module inherits the same safety stack.

✓

✓
DCB0129 / DCB0160

Human in the loop
