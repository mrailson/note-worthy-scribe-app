Not more than to the front gate, maybe thirty yards¹, and even that I'd be terribly out of breath. I use the stick indoors and the wheeled frame if I go outside², not that I go out much now.

Yes, twice this year. Once getting out of the bath, once on the path to the bins³. The second one I was on the floor for I don't know how long before the neighbour came round.

Terrible pain in both knees, worse on the left. Gets worse the longer I'm on my feet⁴. My GP said it's osteoarthritis, I've been on it for years.

Breathlessness?

Very short of breath even walking to the bathroom. I have my blue inhaler⁵. It's the COPD from smoking years ago, silly me.

My daughter takes me, but she has to drop me at the door and park. Last time at the surgery I couldn't make it from the car park and she had to come back for me⁶.

Oh, my daughter's been on at me about that for a year. I never got round to it. The form looked a mile long.

Re: Blue Badge Application — Supporting Medical Evidence for Mrs Dorothy Pearson

I am writing in support of Mrs Pearson's application for a Blue Badge on the grounds of substantial and permanent difficulty walking. Mrs Pearson is known to this practice with a longstanding diagnosis of bilateral osteoarthritis of the knees and moderate to severe chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

A home-based frailty assessment conducted on 18 April 2026 by the East Northants Neighbourhood AgeWell team recorded the following functional findings:

Walking distance: Mrs Pearson is unable to walk more than approximately 27 metres (30 yards) without stopping to rest. She is obliged to pause and steady herself against nearby surfaces within her own home.

Walking aids: Uses a walking stick indoors and a four-wheeled walker outdoors. Mobility outside the home is not possible without aids.

Pain: Severe bilateral knee pain on walking, worse on the left, which deteriorates with the duration of ambulation.

Breathlessness: MRC dyspnoea grade 4 — breathless after walking short distances on level ground. Uses short-acting bronchodilator therapy.

Falls: Two falls within the preceding twelve months, including one "long lie" event with delayed assistance. Falls Risk Assessment Tool (FRAT) score indicates high risk.

Functional impact: On her most recent attendance at the surgery, Mrs Pearson was unable to walk from the car park to the building and had to be returned home by her daughter.

In my clinical opinion, these findings are consistent with a permanent and substantial difficulty in walking that cannot reasonably be expected to improve, and which would be significantly mitigated by the award of a Blue Badge. I would be grateful if her application could be considered favourably.

For review: Mrs Sarah Pearson (daughter & carer)

About Mum's mobility

Mum is 84 and lives on her own in Towcester. I've been trying to get her to apply for a Blue Badge for over a year. This is what I see every time I take her anywhere.

At home, she can't walk from her chair to the kitchen without stopping and holding onto the furniture. It's the same going to the bathroom. She uses a walking stick indoors and she won't go out at all without her wheeled walker.

Outside, thirty yards is her absolute limit — that's the length of her front path. By the time she reaches the gate she is out of breath and in pain. She has severe arthritis in both knees and COPD, and the two combined mean that any distance on foot is genuinely unsafe.

She has had two falls this year. One of them she was on the floor for a long time before a neighbour found her. I worry constantly.

The Blue Badge would make a real difference. At her last GP appointment, she couldn't walk from the surgery car park to the front door, and I had to drive back round and collect her. At the supermarket I have to drop her at the entrance and park separately. A Blue Badge would let her be dropped closer to where she needs to go, and in the winter it would let her keep seeing her grandchildren, which she is gradually stopping doing because of this.

The home visit team from AgeWell helped put this application together from the visit they did on 18 April. The medical details are also in the GP letter attached.

Signed: _____________________ (Sarah Pearson, daughter)
