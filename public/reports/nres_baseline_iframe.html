Complex Care / Long-Term Conditions Service

⚠️ LTC Metrics — Service Model Dependency

Michael's note highlights: "it would be helpful to develop a clearer understanding of the planned approach and what data will be monitored. We want to ensure equity across all patient groups is factored in from the outset, and that outcomes aren't disproportionately influenced by a small subset of patients."

The LTC indicators below are largely dependent on cohort definitions being agreed (Pre-Diabetes, End of Life, Post-Hospital Discharge, COPD with Remote Monitoring). Until these are defined and coded in clinical systems, most LTC metrics will remain proxies. The unique patient count requirement (increasing reach, not just volume) requires patient-level data not available in aggregated GPAD exports.

Population Health & Inequalities

📋 Note on Equity & Population Health

Michael's schedule includes a "Population Health and Inequalities" section which is currently blank in the draft. However, his email explicitly raises equity: ensuring outcomes "aren't disproportionately influenced by a small subset of patients" and that "unique patients are increasing". This cross-cuts all LTC metrics above.

Recommendation: NRES should propose to the ICB that all cohort-level reporting (#18) includes a deprivation breakdown (IMD quintile by patient postcode) and demographic split (age band, ethnicity where recorded). This can be extracted from SystmOne demographic data once cohorts are established. Low additional effort on top of cohort setup, but significant value for demonstrating equitable reach.
