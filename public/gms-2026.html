Four threads, two contracts

The substantive changes group into four themes. Money flowing into practices through the new GP Reimbursement Scheme; refreshed workforce rules where ARRS and the new GMS scheme dovetail around CAIP transitions; a brand-new local flexibility mechanism in the DES; and one operational change to childhood immunisations.

Theme 1 · Money flowing into practices

The new GP Reimbursement Scheme

Section 14A of the GMS SFE Principal Directions, inserted by the Amendment Directions 2026. A new payment scheme for the financial year ending 31 March 2027, with three claim routes and a hard cap of £4.57 × adjusted practice population.

Annual scheme cap
£4.57
per adjusted patient
per practice, per year
Three principles shape the cap and how it can be used:
  • Additionality — cannot duplicate another reimbursement, cannot cover absent GPs
  • Practices over 3,500 patients per GP need NHSE pre-approval to participate
  • Entitlement may be transferred between Core Network Members of the same PCN
Route A

New salaried GP

Employing a salaried GP not employed by the contractor in the past 12 months (retirement returnees and leave-cover GPs are excepted). Must meet additionality.

Up to £152,900 / £155,698 London
Route B

Increased sessions

Lifting an existing salaried GP from below 9 sessions per week up to a maximum of 9 sessions. Hourly cap applies; covers salary, ER NI and pension.

Up to £90.61/hr · £92.27 London
Route C

Continuation of CAIP / Test Sites GPs

Where a post was previously funded by Capacity & Access Payments or the PCN Test Sites Programme and that funding has ended or is ending. PCN approval required.

Up to £152,900 / £155,698 London
Claims process — all claims via CQRSL, submitted within 3 months of the end of the month they relate to. Paid in arrears at the end of the following calendar month. Practices fund the wage-cycle gap.

Theme 2 · Workforce — where the two contracts meet

Additionality, the 12-month bar, and CAIP transitions

DES sections 7.3.8 (GPs) and 7.3.9 (general practice nurses) keep the 12-month bar on rehiring through ARRS, but add an explicit carve-out for staff transferring from CAIP-funded or PCN Test Site posts. This is the bridge that lets PCNs continue funding former CAIP roles through ARRS without breaching additionality — and it sits alongside Route C of the new GP Reimbursement Scheme as an alternative route for the same problem.

Restriction stays

The 12-month bar still applies

A PCN cannot claim ARRS reimbursement for a GP substantively employed in any of its Core Network Practices in the previous 12 months. Same restriction applies to nurses, with a "specialist or more senior nursing role" carve-out.

New exemption

CAIP and Test Site transfers exempt

The bar does not apply where the GP, nurse or HCA is transferring into ARRS from a post which, immediately before transfer, was funded through the Capacity & Access Payment or via NHS England's PCN Test Site programme.

How the two routes connect

For a former-CAIP GP post you have two legitimate routes to fund continuation. They cannot both be used for the same role.

Route via DES
ARRS reimbursement

PCN-level claim under DES section 7.3.8 / 7.3.9. The CAIP/Test Site carve-out lifts the 12-month bar. Cap and rates set by Tables 2/3c of the DES.

OR
Route via GMS
GP Reimbursement Scheme · Route C

Practice-level claim under SFE 14A. PCN approval required. Cap up to £152,900 per GP (£155,698 London), drawing from the £4.57 × population pot.

Theme 3 · New flexibility

The Local Variation Arrangement

For the first time, the DES contains a structured mechanism for local flexibility. Until now any deviation from the national specification needed a national DES Variation issued by NHS England, or a separate Supplementary Network Service. The LVA opens a third route — narrowly scoped, ICB-initiated, but capable of varying the contract requirements on the practice itself.

New mechanism
Local Variation
Arrangement
Section 4.10 · 13 paragraphs
Definition at 2.2.25
Five things to know up front:
  • Only the commissioner (your ICB) can submit a Local Variation Arrangement Request
  • NHS England must approve the request — it is not a delegated decision
  • An LVA can only vary sections 7, 8 and 10.1 to 10.5 — nothing else
  • An LVA can only be approved for the current financial year
  • An LVA forms part of the DES — it is not a Supplementary Network Service

Process at a glance

The 4.10 process moves through five formal steps from request to operational variation.

Step 01
ICB request
Commissioner submits a Local Variation Arrangement Request to NHS England (4.10.1–4.10.2). Must identify PCN(s), practices, sections, dates, rationale, and proposed wording.
Step 02
NHSE review
NHS England may request further information (4.10.3) and decides whether the request meets the criteria in the DES Guidance (4.10.4). May approve unconditionally or with conditions.
Step 03
Local Variation Notice
If approved, NHS England issues a written Local Variation Notice (4.10.5) setting out which PCNs, which practices, which sections, dates, conditions and how it interacts with national DES Variations.
Step 04
Practice agreement
Before the LVA takes effect (4.10.6), the ICB must confirm the final list of participating Core Network Practices and obtain evidence each has agreed to the LVA terms.
Step 05
Contract variation
Each participating practice signs a written variation of its primary medical services contract (4.10.10) and the PCN updates its Network Agreement to match (4.10.11).
Conflict rule (4.10.13) — if NHS England issues a national DES Variation during the year that touches a section also varied locally, the national Variation prevails over the LVA unless NHS England issues an amended Local Variation Notice that expressly preserves the local position. PCNs cannot rely on local terms surviving a national change automatically.

Theme 4 · Service delivery

Childhood immunisation call/recall

A small but operationally significant change in the GMS amendment. The 3-year-4-month childhood immunisation moves from "opportunistic or if requested" to active call/recall. Practices need to plan in the new admin and outreach workload.

Was

Opportunistic / if requested

The 3y4m vaccine was offered when the patient or family attended for another reason, or specifically asked. No active outreach obligation on the practice.

Now

Active call/recall

Practices must actively call and recall the eligible cohort. Plan into call/recall lists, admin time, clinical capacity, IIF/QOF tracking and patient communications.

Issues to watch

Twelve practical considerations across both contracts. Tagged by source: GMS for SFE Amendment items, DES for Network Contract DES items, and Both where the two contracts interact.

01

Real-terms cut for CAIP-maxed practices

Where CAIP funded GP posts at higher levels, the £4.57 cap is materially tighter. Route C provides continuity but does not restore the previous envelope. Model against actual CAIP draw-down per practice.

GMS
02

The 12-month additionality rule (GMS side)

Practices cannot rehire a salaried GP let go in the past year — except retirement returnees and leave-cover GPs. Recent restructures may put returning GPs out of scope of the GP Reimbursement Scheme.

GMS
03

3,500 patients per GP gate

NHS England approval required to participate in the GP Reimbursement Scheme where the patient-per-GP ratio is above 3,500. Most practices likely sit above this. Coordinate one PCN-level submission rather than multiple separate ones.

GMS
04

Double-claim risk on CAIP transfers

Same GP cannot be reimbursed under both ARRS (DES 7.3.8) and the GP Reimbursement Scheme Route C (SFE 14A). Decide route per-post and document the decision before submitting either claim.

Both
05

PCN-level transfer needs governance

The GP Reimbursement Scheme allows transfer of entitlement between Core Network Members. Useful for PCN workforce planning, but practices need a Standing Order or Programme Board mechanism to allocate fairly.

GMS
06

Working capital impact

GP Reimbursement Scheme paid in arrears at the end of the calendar month after submission. Practices fund the wage-cycle gap. Worth flagging in PCN finance discussions for smaller members.

GMS
07

Practice-level contract variation required

Once an LVA is approved, every participating practice must enter a written variation of its primary medical services contract (4.10.10). This is not optional and is the practice's own legal obligation.

DES
08

National variations override LVAs

Under 4.10.13, a national DES Variation issued mid-year prevails over any LVA touching the same section unless NHSE issues an amended Local Variation Notice preserving the local terms. PCNs need a watch-and-flag mechanism.

DES
09

LVA single-year horizon

An LVA can only be approved for the current financial year (4.10.2(b)). Workforce or service redesigns dependent on an LVA will need annual re-approval — not a stable platform for multi-year contracts.

DES
10

LVA conditions can revoke effect mid-year

If an LVA is approved subject to a continuing condition (4.10.9) and that condition stops being satisfied, the LVA ceases to have effect. Track conditions actively — don't treat approval as permanent for the year.

DES
11

CQRSL admin burden

Monthly claim cycles for the GP Reimbursement Scheme plus a declaration against each route's principles per claim. Strong candidate for automated declaration templates and a tracker.

GMS
12

Immunisation workload uplift

The 3y4m vaccine moving to call/recall means active outreach. Plan into call-and-recall lists, admin time, and QOF/IIF performance trajectories.
