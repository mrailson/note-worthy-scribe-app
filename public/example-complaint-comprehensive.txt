COMPLAINT TITLE:
===============
Medication Error and Inadequate Response to Patient Safety Concern

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF COMPLAINT:
=================================

I am writing to formally complain about a serious medication error that occurred during my consultation on 18th November 2024, and the inadequate response from practice staff when I raised my concerns about patient safety.

BACKGROUND:
I have been a patient at Oakville Family Practice for over 15 years and have Type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and a history of penicillin allergy, which is clearly documented in my medical records and highlighted with red warning flags.

INCIDENT DETAILS:
During my routine diabetes review appointment with Dr. James Patterson at 2:15 PM, I presented with symptoms of what appeared to be a chest infection - persistent cough, yellow sputum, and mild fever for 3 days.

Without conducting a proper physical examination or reviewing my allergy status, Dr. Patterson immediately prescribed Amoxicillin 500mg three times daily for 7 days. When I questioned him about my penicillin allergy, he dismissed my concerns and said "a little rash won't hurt you."

MEDICATION ERROR:
The prescription was processed by the practice pharmacist, Ms. Rebecca Thompson, who also failed to check my allergy status despite the electronic warning system. I only discovered the error when I collected the prescription from the attached pharmacy and the pharmacist, Mr. David Chen, immediately flagged the dangerous drug interaction.

STAFF RESPONSE:
When I returned to the practice reception to report the error, the receptionist, Mrs. Linda Foster, was dismissive and suggested it was "just a precaution." When I insisted on speaking to the practice manager, Mrs. Jennifer Hayes, I was told she was "too busy" and that I should "just get a different antibiotic from any pharmacy."

SAFETY CONCERNS:
This medication error could have resulted in a severe allergic reaction, including anaphylaxis, which could have been life-threatening. The casual attitude of staff towards patient safety is deeply concerning and suggests systemic issues with the practice's medication management protocols.
